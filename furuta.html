Introdution

The inverted pendulum is a very popular experiment used for educational purposes in modern control theory and this system can appear with different constructions. The Furuta is one such construction.

System definition

The Furuta pendulum has a direct-drive motor as its actuator source and its pendulum attached to the rotating shaft of the motor.Since the angular acceleration of the pole cannot be controlled directly, the Furuta pendulum is an underactuated mechanical system.

Kit objectives

The cart-pole system serves as an ideal platform for learning and applying various engineering and control theory concepts. Its objectives include developing a mathematical model using the Lagrangian approach, linearizing the nonlinear dynamics around a controllable equilibrium, testing controllability, and designing controllers like LQR for state-space regulation. It enables exploration of advanced topics such as passivity and nonlinear control, including guiding the pendulum to its homoclinic orbit and stabilizing it. The system also provides hands-on experience with sensors, microcontrollers, and system design while fostering critical thinking and problem-solving before coding. Additionally, it integrates practical skills like working with ROS 2 for robotics applications and software development, offering a comprehensive platform to bridge theory and real-world engineering challenges.

What you get

The kit includes detailed tutorials covering every aspect of the cart-pole system, from computing its mathematical model to designing and simulating the system in Gazebo. It guides you through solving for both the swing-up and LQR controllers, implementing these controllers on microcontrollers, and interfacing them with ROS 2 for seamless integration. Additionally, the tutorials cover visualizing system data and provide insights into practical applications, making the kit a comprehensive resource for mastering control theory and robotics.
