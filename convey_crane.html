Introdution

The inverted pendulum is one of the most popular laboratory experiments used for illustrating non-linear control techniques. This system is motivated by applications such as the control of rockets and the antiseismic control of buildings.

System definition

The swinging pendulum on a convey-crane consists of a pole with an additional mass at its end, pivoted on a movable platform. This pendulum is free to swing about its pivot point without direct control actuation. The platform can move horizontally, perpendicular to the axis of rotation of the pendulum, and is actuated by a force applied in the same direction. The control objective is to stabilize the pendulum with the extra mass at its upper unstable equilibrium position by moving the platform horizontally. Since the angular acceleration of the pendulum cannot be directly controlled, the convey-crane system is an underactuated mechanical system. As a result, conventional techniques designed for fully actuated mechanical systems, such as robotic manipulators, cannot be directly applied to control this system.

Kit objectives

The convey-crane system provides an excellent platform for exploring a wide range of engineering and control theory concepts. Its objectives include developing a mathematical model using the Lagrangian approach, linearizing the nonlinear dynamics around a controllable equilibrium point, testing the system's controllability, and designing controllers like LQR for state-space regulation. It also allows for the exploration of advanced topics such as passivity and nonlinear control, including guiding the pendulum with the extra mass to its homoclinic orbit and stabilizing it. Additionally, the system offers hands-on experience with sensors, microcontrollers, and system design while fostering critical thinking and problem-solving skills. With practical integration of robotics tools like ROS 2 and real-world software development, the convey-crane system is a comprehensive platform for mastering control systems and engineering fundamentals.
